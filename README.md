An app which gamifies the process of recycling as well as teaching you on the
spot about different types of plastics and recyclability.

> [!NOTE]
> Did you know that less than 10% of plastics are recycled?[^1]

[^1]: [Plastics for Change blog report](https://www.plasticsforchange.org/blog/why-is-less-than-10-of-the-worlds-plastic-being-recycled).

## Motivation

There's a lot of information about ecology, but it's not something that's
integrated in our daily lives to an extent where it changes our daily lives.

A lot of products, such as shampoo bottles have several different types of
plastic, each with differing melting points making it hard to recycle.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Not all plastics are recyclable.[^2]

[^2]: [CORDIS News report on recyclability of plastics](https://cordis.europa.eu/article/id/461627-why-isn-t-all-plastic-recyclable).

This makes it all the more valuable to have access to what types of plastics
are actually right to put in recycle bins, instead of blindly putting anything
that's "plastic" in them!

## The Solution

Have everything we need at the spot available for access within the tips of our
hands in a few seconds.

Lots of people have:

- Not enough time

- An open mind

- Willingness to learn about recyclability and help the environment

But end up feeling too much friction from doing it on the spot, they have work,
or something else they're focused on— and that's the type of friction that
contributes to a huge gap of adaptivity.

### Examples

Here's an example: [Dual Flushing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dual_flush_toilet).

A lot of people didn't know about which flush does what[^3] when it first
released. So it was nudged in restrooms for people to know which button does
what in order to spread awareness about the conservation of water.

A [Netherlands University of Twente](https://www.utwente.nl/en/) study has
found that this nudging produced noticeable results, as the maximum percentage
of water reduction achieved became 10.7%, and an average of 6.17%.[^4]

[^3]: [Water conservation community forum post](https://www.plumbingtalks.com/community/water-conservation-regulations/do-you-pay-attention-to-water-saving-rules-when-fixing-stuff-at-home/paged/32/?foro=allread&foro_n=47cb6777a6).

[^4]: [Blog post about a student's study at Twente](https://www.utwente.nl/en/sustainability/sustainability-news/2023/7/1035344/innovative-nudging-intervention-pilot-reduces-toilet-water-waste).
